Training Plans

Couch to 5K: An 8-Week Plan

Inspired by the "Start to Run" program, which has been shown to effectively increase physical activity among novice runners[1].

Start to run is typically a program where practice will occur 3 times per week, with rest days between.

  1. Week 1: Walk 5 min, Run 1 min (Repeat 4 times)
  2. Week 2: Walk 4 min, Run 2 min (Repeat 4 times)
  3. Week 3: Walk 3 min, Run 3 min (Repeat 4 times)
  4. Week 4: Walk 2 min, Run 4 min (Repeat 4 times)
  5. Week 5: Walk 2 min, Run 5 min (Repeat 3 times)
  6. Week 6: Walk 1 min, Run 6 min (Repeat 3 times)
  7. Week 7: Walk 1 min, Run 7 min (Repeat 3 times)
  8. Week 8: Walk 1 min, Run 8 min (Repeat 3 times)

Marathon Training: An 18-Week Plan for Serious Runners

Based on research, this plan is designed for those who are serious about completing a marathon and have some running experience[5].

  1. Week 1-3: Base Building
    • Long Run: 10 miles
    • Midweek Runs: 4-6 miles
    • Total Weekly Mileage: 20-25 miles
  2. Week 4-6: Endurance and Strength
    • Long Run: 14 miles
    • Midweek Runs: 6-8 miles with hill repeats
    • Total Weekly Mileage: 30-35 miles
  3. Week 7-9: Speed Work
    • Long Run: 18 miles
    • Midweek Runs: 7-9 miles with intervals
    • Total Weekly Mileage: 40-45 miles
  4. Week 10-12: Peak Training
    • Long Run: 22 miles
    • Midweek Runs: 8-10 miles with tempo runs
    • Total Weekly Mileage: 50-55 miles
  5. Week 13-15: Tapering
    • Long Run: 16 miles
    • Midweek Runs: 6-8 miles easy pace
    • Total Weekly Mileage: 35-40 miles
  6. Week 16-18: Race Preparation
    • Long Run: 12 miles
    • Midweek Runs: 4-6 miles at a moderate pace
    • Total Weekly Mileage: 25-30 miles

Most Common Running-Related Injuries and Prevention

  1. Stress Fractures

    One of the most common and potentially serious overuse injuries, especially in weight-bearing activities like running. The tibia is the bone most commonly injured among runners[6].

    • Prevention: Gradual increase in training intensity and proper footwear.
  2. Mechanical Low Back Pain

    Another common issue, particularly in high-impact activities[7].

    • Prevention: Core strengthening exercises and proper running form.
  3. Musculoskeletal Injuries

    Prevalent in various military occupations, including those that involve running[7].

    • Prevention: Different injury prevention strategies may be needed across different types of running activities.
  4. Overuse Injuries

    Common in sports that require a combination of physical fitness, skill, and strategy[8].

    • Prevention: Adequate rest between training sessions and cross-training.
